Influencer Marketing for Pickleball Brand

Outreach to 40 micro-influencers for emerging pickleball brand to build awareness through commission-based partnerships.

Client Sports Equipment Brand (Pickleball)
Category Influencer Marketing
Duration 3 Months
Role SEO Executive

Overview

Emerging pickleball equipment brand seeking to build brand awareness through authentic influencer partnerships on a commission-only model.

Challenge

Emerging pickleball equipment brand with:

  • Zero brand awareness in Indian sports market
  • No budget for paid influencer collaborations
  • Commission-only partnership model (25-35% of sales)
  • Need to build credibility and social proof
  • Competing with established badminton/tennis brands

Key Constraint: Brand unwilling to send free products for testing. Insisted on commission-only model where influencers would purchase themselves or earn commission on sales. This made partnership negotiations extremely difficult.

Strategy & Approach

Phase 1: Influencer Research

Identified 40+ micro-influencers using:

  • Instagram hashtag analysis: #pickleball #pickleballlife #pickleballindia #racketsports
  • Checked who was posting about pickleball regularly
  • Analyzed competitor brand collaborations
  • Evaluated engagement rates (minimum 3% required)
  • Assessed audience demographics and content quality

Selection Criteria

  • Follower count: 5K-50K (micro-influencer sweet spot)
  • Engagement rate: 3%+ (indicates authentic audience)
  • Content quality: High-quality photos/videos, consistent posting
  • Niche alignment: Sports, fitness, racquet sports enthusiasts
  • Audience demographics: Active lifestyle, sports participation, 25-45 age range

Influencer Tracking

Created spreadsheet documenting:

  • Influencer name and handle
  • Follower count and engagement rate
  • Content style and posting frequency
  • Contact information (email, DM availability)
  • Niche relevance score
  • Previous brand collaborations

Phase 2: Outreach Strategy

Developed personalized outreach approach:

  • No generic templates - each message customized
  • Mentioned specific posts I appreciated
  • Explained brand story and product benefits
  • Proposed partnership terms clearly upfront
  • Made it easy to say yes with simple next steps

Outreach Message Structure

  1. Personal greeting referencing their content
  2. Brief brand introduction (who we are, what we offer)
  3. Why I thought they'd be a good fit (specific reasons)
  4. Partnership proposal (commission structure or product exchange)
  5. Clear next step (reply to discuss details)

Phase 3: Partnership Negotiation

Proposed two models:

  • Model A: Commission-only (25-35% of sales from their unique code)
  • Model B: Product exchange (free paddle for honest review + commission on future sales)

Challenge: Brand insisted on Model A only. This created major friction because:

  • Influencers want to test products before recommending to audience
  • Commission-only feels risky (what if product is poor quality?)
  • Established norm in influencer marketing is product exchange
  • Without product in hand, influencers can't create authentic content

Execution

  • Reached out to 40 micro-influencers via Instagram DMs and email
  • Personalized each outreach message with specific content references
  • Followed up 2-3 times with non-responders (polite, value-add follow-ups)
  • Negotiated with interested influencers on partnership terms
  • Coordinated between brand and influencers on commission setup

Why Partnerships Didn't Close

Despite strong interest, partnerships fell through because:

  • Influencers wanted to test product before promoting (standard practice)
  • Commission-only model perceived as too risky
  • Brand unwilling to invest even ₹2,000-3,000 per influencer for product samples
  • Lack of trust from brand side ("they'll take free product and not post")
  • Influencers concerned about recommending untested product to their audience

Campaign Assets

Outreach messages, influencer spreadsheets, and negotiation samples

Key Learnings

  • Product samples are essential - Influencers need to test before recommending; commission-only models have 80-90% rejection rates
  • Micro-influencers deliver better ROI - Higher engagement, more authentic content, better audience trust than macro-influencers
  • Personalization matters - Generic templates ignored; customized outreach got 25% response rate
  • Trust is two-way - Brands must trust influencers; influencers must trust product quality
  • Hybrid model works best - Free product for initial review + commission on ongoing sales = win-win

Recommended Model

  • Send free product (₹2,000-3,000 investment per influencer)
  • Request honest review (1 post + 2-3 stories)
  • Offer 15-20% ongoing commission on sales from their code
  • Build long-term relationships rather than transactional one-offs

What I Would Do Differently

  • Advocate more strongly to brand for product exchange model
  • Start with 2-3 influencers (proof of concept) before scaling
  • Create influencer contract templates clarifying expectations
  • Set up trackable discount codes before outreach begins
  • Build relationships over time rather than immediate partnership asks
