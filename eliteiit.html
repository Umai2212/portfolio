Full-Stack Digital Marketing for Elite IIT Coaching

Joined as sole digital marketing manager for a 4-branch coaching institute in Bangalore — audited a broken setup, launched campaigns across Meta Ads, Google Ads, SEO, and GBP, and delivered 454 leads at ₹51.11 CPL in the first 30 days.

ClientElite IIT Coaching, Bangalore
RoleDigital Marketing Manager
PeriodMay 2026 – Present
ChannelsMeta Ads · Google Ads · SEO · GBP · WordPress

The Situation

Elite IIT runs 4 branches across Bangalore — Yelahanka, Jayanagar, Koramangala, and Malleshwaram — offering JEE, NEET, and Class 3–12 tuition. They had been using freelancers and hired a full-time manager for the first time.

When I joined: Meta Ads running with no qualification filters and an inflated lead count, Google Ads conversion tracking broken since launch, GBP profiles at position 16+ city-wide, the highest-selling product (Class 3–12 tuition) with zero digital presence, and an internal narrative that performance had dropped — based on numbers that turned out to be misleading.

What I Walked Into

  • 18-month Meta Ads average CPL of ₹65.91 — inflated by unqualified leads
  • Facebook page restricted — 97% delivery on Instagram only
  • Google Ads showing 14 form submissions with zero emails received — tracking broken since launch
  • NEET page at rank ~24 with 2,200 words and 2 organic visits/month
  • GBP profiles unoptimised — Class 3–12 tuition missing across all 4 branches
  • No leads tracking, no reporting infrastructure
  • Website loading in 7 seconds — 1,160 impressions/day but only 14 clicks

What Was Expected

  • Lower CPL than the previous freelancer's ₹53.37
  • More qualified leads reaching the counseling team
  • Local SEO visibility across all 4 branches
  • Proper tracking and reporting with real numbers
  • A digital marketing function that runs independently

Meta Ads

The previous setup had a single NEET campaign with no segmentation and no qualification layer. The June 2025 numbers being compared internally included Navodaya, School Tuition, and job applicant leads all counted as "NEET leads." The comparison was inaccurate.

I restructured the account: separate campaigns per product, geo-targeted audiences per branch, and a WhatsApp qualification filter so only relevant leads reached the counseling team. CPL came in below the freelancer benchmark while quality improved.

Google Ads — Tracking Fix

Rakesh flagged at 8:55 PM on June 10 that Google Ads was showing 14 form submissions but no emails had been received. I responded at 5:01 AM June 11 and had the root cause diagnosed and fixed by 12:04 PM — 15 hours total.

Root Cause

The lead form asset was collecting submissions but no conversion event was firing and email notifications had never been configured. The ads were spending with no optimisation signal going back to Google.

Fix

  • Implemented Thank You page redirect as the conversion signal
  • Configured Google Tag Manager to fire the conversion event on thank you page load
  • Set test campaign at ₹200/day to verify end-to-end before scaling
  • Conversion tracking confirmed live — Google Ads now has a proper optimisation signal

This had been broken since the account launched. Nobody had caught it until the flag came in.

SEO & Google Business Profile

NEET Page — From Invisible to Page 1

The NEET page was getting 2 organic visits a month with 2,200 words of thin content at rank ~24. I rebuilt it: grew to 4,800 words with hub-and-spoke structure, added FAQ schema markup, built internal links from related pages. The page was heading toward page 1 within the month.

Malleshwaram — Ranked From Zero

No location page existed and the GBP profile was at position 16+ city-wide. I built a dedicated location page, optimised the GBP profile, and the combination moved Malleshwaram to rank #2 on Google Search and #4 on Google Maps — within one month.

Search Console Insight

The site was appearing 1,160 times/day in search results but generating only 14 clicks. Traced to a 7-second page load time from plugin bloat — the SEO was working but the site was losing the traffic. Root cause documented and flagged for fix.

Infrastructure Built

None of the following were requested. Built proactively because they didn't exist.

  • Leads tracking sheet — full pipeline visibility shared with the whole team
  • 18-month historical campaign analysis — corrected the internal narrative that performance had dropped
  • WhatsApp qualification filter — screens leads before the counseling team, improving conversion quality by an estimated 20–30%
  • Keyword research sheet — delivered same day as requested
  • Monthly execution calendar — shared with Rakesh for visibility
  • Geo-targeting circles — Yelahanka and Koramangala built in Google Maps for precise ad targeting
  • EOD reporting system — daily report sent every evening covering actions, movement, flags, and next day focus

Key Learnings

  • Audit before you optimise — The internal numbers looked bad because the comparison was wrong. Building the 18-month analysis first meant every decision was based on accurate data
  • Qualification matters more than volume — Removing unqualified leads from the pipeline is worth more than adding more leads. The counseling team's time is the real constraint
  • Speed beats perfection under deadline — The crash course campaign had to move same day. Copy in-house, creatives approved, landing page live in 48 hours
  • Tracking is the foundation — The Google Ads account had been spending with zero conversion signal for weeks. You cannot optimise what you cannot measure
  • Local SEO compounds fast — One month of focused GBP + location page work moved Malleshwaram from invisible to #2. The other 3 branches follow the same playbook
  • Averages hide the real story — A blended CPL or cost-per-result number looks fine until you break it down by campaign. Frequency creeping above 2.0 on the Class 6–12 ad was the early signal of audience fatigue that the average alone wouldn't have shown
